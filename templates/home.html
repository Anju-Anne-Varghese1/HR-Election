US House of Representatives Election

INTRODUCTION

The House of Representatives is made up of 435 elected members, divided among the 50 states in proportion to their total population. In addition, there are 6 non-voting members, representing the District of Columbia, the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, and four other territories of the United States: American Samoa, Guam, the U.S. Virgin Islands, and the Commonwealth of Northern Mariana Islands. Members of the House are elected every two years. Congressional elections determine who represents the state in the U.S. House of Representatives. They also decide which political party will hold a majority in each chamber of Congress for the next two years. Since these midterm elections are important for the future of the country, the model was created to predict the election results of the year 2024 using data from both 1976 to 2020, 2022 elections, and 2024.

DATA

The data regarding the house of representatives were taken from Harvard Dataverse maintained by the MIT Election Data and Science Lab. The data had 31103 rows and 19 columns. The column included the year of the election, state, district, party, number of votes of the candidates, total votes received during the election, etc.

DECISION TREE MODEL

The model was tuned to get the optimum hyperparameters. The model achieved 91.16 % accuracy on the training set (election results of 1978-2020) and 88.28 % accuracy on the validation set (election results of 2022).

CONCLUSION

We chose Decision tree as the final model for predicting the election results for the year 2024. The model had 91.16 % accuracy on the training set (election results of 1978-2020) and 88.28 % accuracy on the validation set (election results of 2022). Out of the 435 house seats, the model correctly predicted 384 of them, with many of the wrong predictions coming from California, and Florida. California, and Florida have 15 and 6 districts incorrectly predicted, with these two states alone making up nearly 41% of the wrong predictions.
